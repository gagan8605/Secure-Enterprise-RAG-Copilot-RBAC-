COMPENSATION PHILOSOPHY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

TechCorp targets the 65th percentile of the San Francisco Bay Area market for
base salary and the 75th percentile when including total compensation (base +
equity + bonus). Market data sourced from Radford Global Technology Survey,
Levels.fyi, and Carta Compensation Benchmarking (Q1 2025 data).

All offers must fall within the applicable band. Exceptions above band maximum
require CPO + CEO approval. No exceptions will be made for above-max offers
without written approval on file.

Notes:
  • Equity grants vest over 4 years: 25% cliff at 12 months, then monthly.
  • Refresh grants are awarded annually at the Q4 performance review cycle.
  • IC-1 and IC-2 are not eligible for equity refresh in the first 2 years.
  • Salaries above represent the Austin, TX benchmark. SF/NYC = +15–20% geo adjustment.
  • Remote employees outside major metros may have a geo adjustment of -5% to -15%.

ANNUAL REVIEW & MERIT CYCLE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  • Annual performance reviews: November (peer + manager review)
  • Merit increase decisions: December, effective January 1
  • Merit budget for FY2025: 5% of total salary spend
  • Distribution guidance:
    - Exceeds Expectations: 7–10% merit increase
    - Meets Expectations:   3–5% merit increase
    - Below Expectations:   0% (performance improvement plan may apply)
  • Promotions: Require manager nomination + band calibration committee approval.
    Promotion increase = new band midpoint or minimum 8% above current salary.
